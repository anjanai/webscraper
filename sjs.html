Classics Season
Let The Trumpet Sound
October 1, 2022 8:00 pm
Buy Tickets
October 2, 2022 2:30 pm
Buy Tickets

What better way to open our season than with the trumpet? Renowned on six continents, Norwegian soloist Tine Thing Helseth performs Tomasi’s formidable Trumpet Concerto with her signature lyricism and warmth. The concert opens with Rimsky-Korsakov’s blazing Russian Easter Overture and is capped by Schumann’s sunny Symphony No. 3, ending in triumphant fanfares for brass.

Performances

  • Nikolai Rimsky-Korsakov   Russian Easter Overture

  • Maurice Ravel   Le Tombeau de Couperin

  • Henri Tomasi   Trumpet Concerto

  • Robert Schumann   Symphony No. 3 Rhenish

Conductor

Andrés Cárdenes (Bio)

Soloist

Tine Thing Helseth (Bio)

La Grand Viola
October 22, 2022 8:00 pm
Buy Tickets
October 23, 2022 2:30 pm
Buy Tickets

With its deep, resonant sound, the viola is known as the orchestra’s inner voice; but its solo magic is equally powerful. Dazzling English violist Timothy Ridout, one of Europe’s foremost young stars, brings us two contrasting works that will leave listeners marveling. Our program begins with a high-spirited early piece by Afro-English composer Coleridge-Taylor, and Mendelssohn’s joyful “Italian” Symphony brings it to a close.

Performances

  • Samuel Coleridge-Taylor  Ballade in A minor

  • Ralph Vaughan Williams  Suite for Viola

  • Niccolò Paganini  Sonata per la Grand Viola

  • Felix Mendelssohn  Symphony No. 4 Italian

Conductor

Tito Muñoz (Bio)

Soloist

Timothy Ridout, Viola (Bio)

Mighty Cello
December 3, 2022 8:00 pm
Buy Tickets
December 4, 2022 2:30 pm
Buy Tickets

Cellist Julian Schwarz, already near-legendary in his early 30’s (“Stunning– has to be heard to be believed!”) marks his second appearance in San Jose with one of his favorite works, Lalo’s Concerto in D minor. Maestra Falletta opens the concert with Turina’s infectious Danzas, filled with Spanish rhythms and color; and concludes with Dvorák’s masterful, melody-rich Symphony No.7.

Performances

  • Joaquín Turina  Danzas fantásticas

  • Edouard Lalo  Cello Concerto

  • Antonín Dvorák  Symphony No. 7

Conductor

JoAnn Falletta (Bio)

Soloist

Julian Schwarz, cello (Bio)

Tchaikovsky Flute
January 21, 2023 8:00 pm
Buy Tickets
January 22, 2022 2:30 pm
Buy Tickets

Last season the Symphony first welcomed world-renowned flutist Denis Bouriakov. With brilliant artistry, Bouriakov adds to the flute’s repertory by identifying and transcribing works that will allow it to shine; and he returns this year to perform Tchaikovsky’s magnificent Violin Concerto – on the flute! Stravinsky’s Chant du Rossignol introduces our concert, followed by Sibelius’ final, greatest symphony.

Performances

  • Igor Stravinsky  Chant du Rossignol

  • Jean Sibelius  Symphony No. 7

  • Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky  Violin Concerto arranged for Flute

Conductor

Jayce Ogren (Bio)

Soloist

Denis Bouriakov, flute (Bio)

Nakamatsu Plays Grieg
March 18, 2023 8:00 pm
Buy Tickets
March 19, 2023 2:30 pm
Buy Tickets

San Jose’s own Cliburn gold medal winner Jon Nakamatsu joins our season of stars to reprise the first work he ever played with Symphony San Jose: Grieg’s Piano Concerto. (A critic once noted that the work ‘brought smile after smile to Nakamatsu’s face.’) The program ends with Florence Price’s pioneering Symphony No. 1, skillfully incorporating spirituals, folksong and syncopated African American dance rhythms into a classical structure.

Performances

  • David Amram  Jhaptal

  • Edvard Grieg  Piano Concerto

  • Florence Price  Symphony No. 1

Conductor

Aram Demirjian (Bio)

Soloist

Jon Nakamatsu, piano (Bio)

Brahms & Fandango
May 6, 2023 8:00 pm
Buy Tickets
May 7, 2023 2:30 pm
Buy Tickets

Violin virtuoso Anne Akiko Meyers returns to us with a new commission from preeminent Mexican composer Arturo Marquez. Her request for a concerto based on Mariachi traditions reflected his father’s heritage, and so was born the fiery, soulful Fandango. Last summer’s premiere electrified the audience; Meyers calls it simply ‘a blockbuster.’ Bracketing it with Finzi’s lyrical Prelude and Brahms’ Symphony No. 4, we offer a treat for Spring.

Performances

  • Gerald Finzi: Prelude for String Orchestra in F minor

  • Arturo Marquez: Fandango

  • Brahms: Symphony No. 4

Conductor

Tatsuya Shimono (Bio)

Soloist

Anne Akiko Meyers, Violin (Bio)

Carmina Burana
June 3, 2023 8:00 pm
Buy Tickets
June 4, 2023 2:30 pm
Buy Tickets

Lyric soprano Maria Valdes, “a star in the making,” opens the Symphony’s final program as soloist in Samuel Barber’s haunting masterpiece, Knoxville Summer of 1915. Added soloists, three choirs and the full orchestra then join her in Carl Orff’s exuberant cantata Carmina Burana, based on medieval poems and songs celebrating the sensual life, deploring fate – and bringing our 2022-23 season to a rousing close.

Performances

  • Samuel Barber: Knoxville Summer of 1915

  • Carl Orff: Carmina Burana

Conductor

Carlos Vieu (Bio)

Soloist

Maria Valdes, Soprano (Bio)

February 12, 2023 2:30 pm

Young Salvatore Di Vita discovers a refuge from life in his war-torn Sicilian village: its Cinema Paradiso movie house, where projectionist Alfredo instills in the boy a deep love of films. Then Salvatore grows up…This beloved film (with a Rotten Tomatoes audience rating of 97) features one of the most unforgettable of all movie scores, by Italian maestro Ennio Morricone. Prepare to fall in love all over again.

Amadeus Live
February 12, 2022 8:00 pm
Buy Tickets
February 13, 2022 2:30 pm
Buy Tickets

Antonio Salieri believes that Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart’s music is divine, miraculous…“damn it!” Mozart comes alive in all his mischief and glory in Miloš Foreman’s fanciful film-biography. Its prize-winning score, including portions of The Magic Flute, the Requiem in D minor, Don Giovanni, Symphony No 25 in G minor and much much more, is ‘one of the most popular classical music recordings of all time.’ Hear it performed live by Symphony San Jose and Chorale, as you watch the film unfold on the big screen.

Special Events
Strike Up The Band!
September 4, 2021 7:00 pm
September 5, 2021 5:30 pm

Two glorious concerts — absolutely FREE — on San Jose State’s Tower Lawn

Symphony Silicon Valley and Opera San Jose team up to present a free outdoor musical extravaganza to celebrate Labor Day weekend, and to welcome Live Music back to our community. These 75 to 90-minute programs will feature full symphony orchestra, operatic soloists, and the piano instrumental magic of San Jose’s very own Jon Nakamatsu, Gold Medalist of the Van Cliburn International Piano Competition. The programs, under the baton of conductor Peter Jaffe, will present the music of Gershwin, Sibelius, Mozart, Puccini, Brahms, Tchaikovsky and more. There will be some chairs and water bottles provided, but you can bring your own lawn chair or blanket, as well as a picnic dinner.
